The best free VST plugins to install first

For most beginners, the smartest path is to build a balanced toolkit. Start with one versatile synth, one source of realistic instruments, one drum plugin, a few dependable mixing tools, and one metering plugin. That gives you enough to write, arrange, mix, and finish songs without feeling overwhelmed.

1. Vital

Vital is one of the strongest free synth plugins because it covers an enormous range of sounds. You can make basses, leads, pads, plucks, and motion-rich textures from one instrument. It is especially useful if you enjoy software instruments and want to learn synthesis at the same time.

Official Vital download

2. Spitfire LABS

LABS is ideal when you want inspiring sounds quickly. The interface is simple, but the musical results can feel polished and emotional. It is a great choice for adding pianos, strings, felt textures, or subtle cinematic layers to a track without spending money.

Official LABS download

3. MT Power Drum Kit 2

If you write songs on keyboard or guitar, a drum plugin can instantly make your ideas feel more complete. MT Power Drum Kit 2 is popular because it is straightforward and musical. It gives you a realistic acoustic drum option when you do not want to program every hit from scratch.

Official MT Power Drum Kit 2 download

4. TDR Nova

TDR Nova is one of the best free mixing plugins because it does more than normal EQ. You can use it to tame harsh vocals, control boxiness in guitars, or reduce mud in a busy mix in a more intelligent way. It is especially good for learners because it helps you hear why certain frequencies need attention.

Official TDR Nova page

5. Valhalla Supermassive

For space, atmosphere, and width, Valhalla Supermassive is excellent. It is not just a basic reverb. It is the kind of plugin that can turn plain sounds into something more cinematic or emotional. If you make ambient, synth-based, or textured music, it is worth installing early.

Official Supermassive page

6. Youlean Loudness Meter

Metering often gets ignored by beginners, but it matters. If your mixes are too loud, too quiet, or inconsistent, a loudness meter helps you make better decisions. This is especially useful when you start uploading tracks or comparing your work against commercial releases.

Official Youlean page

How to choose the right free plugins for your setup

The best plugin is not always the one with the most features. It is the one that helps you finish music. If you mainly write songs, focus on pianos, drums, synths, and a simple reverb. If you mainly mix, prioritise EQ, compression, saturation, and metering. If you are still learning REAPER, avoid downloading too much at once. A small setup is easier to understand and use well.

  • Choose a synth if you want modern electronic sounds and basses.
  • Choose sampled instruments if you want pianos, strings, or organic textures.
  • Choose drum plugins if you need faster arrangement and demo creation.
  • Choose EQ and compression tools if your mixes feel muddy, harsh, or flat.
  • Choose a loudness meter if you want more confidence when exporting final tracks.

Frequently asked questions

Are free VST plugins good enough for serious music production?

Yes. Many free plugins are more than good enough for learning, demo creation, content production, and even finished releases. Paid plugins often add workflow improvements, more presets, bigger libraries, or extra polish, but free tools can absolutely get you started properly.

Do free VST plugins work in REAPER on Windows and Mac?

Many do, but always check the plugin format and operating system support before installing. REAPER users commonly use VST3 and AU on Mac, and VST2 or VST3 on Windows depending on the plugin and setup.

Should I install lots of plugins at once?

No. Start with a focused set. Too many options can slow you down. Learn a few well, then expand when you know what is missing from your workflow.
