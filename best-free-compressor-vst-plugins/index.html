What compression actually does

Compression reduces the difference between louder and quieter moments. That can make a vocal easier to hear, a bass steadier, or drums feel more controlled.

The goal is usually not to squash everything. It is to make important parts feel more reliable in the mix.

How beginners should use compressors

Start gently. Listen for steadiness, not obvious pumping.

A little compression on the right track is usually more effective than heavy compression everywhere.

Where free compressors work well

Free compressors are excellent for vocals, bass, drum control, acoustic guitar levelling, and mix-bus practice.

They are also a great way to learn hearing skills before paying for premium analog-style tools.
